Shri Muttuswami Dikshitar

Exploring the life and legacy of a Carnatic music legend

About Shri Muttuswami Dikshitar

Muttuswami Dikshitar (1775â€“1835) was one of the greatest composers of Carnatic music, belonging to the legendary Trinity of Carnatic music alongside Tyagaraja and Shyama Shastri. Born in Tiruvarur, Tamil Nadu, Dikshitar was deeply immersed in music from childhood, trained by his father, Ramaswami Dikshitar. His compositions are unique because they are primarily in Sanskrit, infused with complex lyrical structures and deep philosophical meaning. His works are heavily influenced by North Indian music traditions and are often set in slow tempo, emphasizing melodic depth. Dikshitar was known for composing in a wide range of ragas, many of which were introduced or revived by him. He created an extensive collection of kritis, varnams, and other compositions, including the famous **Navagraha Kritis**, which are dedicated to the nine planetary deities, and the **Kamalamba Navavarna Kritis**, based on the Sri Chakra and Devi worship. Some of his most celebrated kritis include **"Vatapi Ganapatim Bhajeham" (Hamsadhwani raga)**, **"Meenakshi Me Mudam Dehi" (Purvikalyani raga)**, and **"Sri Subramanyaya Namaste" (Kamboji raga)**. His music reflects deep devotion and a mastery of both melody and rhythm. Unlike his contemporary Tyagaraja, who composed mainly in Telugu, Dikshitar wrote in Sanskrit, often incorporating elements of Hindustani music into his compositions. His music remains a vital part of Carnatic concerts and continues to inspire musicians worldwide.
